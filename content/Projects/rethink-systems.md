> The parent company behind everything — community-led SaaS, education, and the middleware of the startup ecosystem.

## Context

**Rethink Systems** is the umbrella Shravan founded in 2024 after leaving Bhanzu. It is not one product — it is the platform that ties together the teaching, the building, and the community. Every sub-project — [[rethink-dashboard]], [[builders-bible]], [[the-swagwala-pm]], [[prabhupada-ai]] — lives under this roof. The thesis is community-first: build a community, then build products with and for that community.

## Details

### What It Is

Rethink Systems operates as a community-led SaaS and education platform with two distinct arms. The first is **ed-tech** — education platforms, cohort programs, and free resources like the Builder's Bible. The second is **Talent-as-a-Service**, connecting trained professionals with startup opportunities. Shravan describes the company as "the middleware of the startup ecosystem," sitting between the talent and the companies that need it.

The registered company name is **Naum Infinitium Private Limited**, incorporated in February 2024 and based in Bengaluru. It is bootstrapped and unfunded — growth has been entirely organic. An earlier community called "All Things Product" predates or ran alongside the @TheSwagWalaPM branding.

### Scale

As of April 2026, the numbers tell a clear story: **194K+ LinkedIn followers**, **45K+ YouTube subscribers**, and over **$1M in revenue** generated through 0-1 startups. More than **10,000 product managers** have been mentored, up from an earlier figure of 500+. The cohort programs have drawn **1,600+ applications**, and **20+ video testimonials** from graduates provide social proof. Notable alumni have gone on to roles at Google, Amazon, Microsoft, IBM, Boeing, Salesforce, Flipkart, Mastercard, Airtel Payments Bank, Zomato, Stripe, Oracle, Nagarro, Barclays, ADP, and TCS.

Among the most prominent testimonials are **Varun Khanna** (Product Lead at Google, 12+ YOE), **Isha Singal** (Sr. Technical PM at Amazon, 17+ YOE), **Kevin Thomas** (Senior AI PM at IBM, 5+ YOE), **Deepak Poddar** (Director Global PM at Mastercard, 20+ YOE), and **Puneet Mehra** (VP at Barclays, 21+ YOE).

### Flagship Product: Mastering Product Management 2.0

The centerpiece offering is **Mastering Product Management 2.0**, an 8-week live cohort-based course focused on AI-first execution, design thinking, and real-world building. Each week includes co-building sessions, and the curriculum emphasizes first-principles product thinking, AI tool mastery, product decision-making, and practical execution. The course costs $1,000 per participant.

The first cohort attracted 100 participants from Estonia to Croatia and generated **$150K+ in revenue with zero customer acquisition cost** — all from organic community. The 8th cohort is set to launch on September 27, 2026, under the tagline "Built for Everyone."

### Cohort Philosophy

Shravan holds a firm belief: "You cannot get quality education in any cohort greater than 25 people." This hard cap on cohort size drives a **gurukul-inspired scaling model** — develop 50 mentors who each train 20 educators. The strategy is to scale through people, not class size, preserving the intimacy that makes the education transformative.

### Instructor Positioning

The rethinksystems.in website positions Shravan with 10+ years building products across logistics, edtech, and consumer tech. He is a guest lecturer at IIMs, IITs, ISB, and NTU, and an angel investor in 20+ startups.

### Sub-Projects

The Rethink ecosystem includes several interconnected projects: **Rethink Dashboard** for ed-tech platform infrastructure (see [[rethink-dashboard]]), the **Builder's Bible** as the 700+ page free book at build.rethinksystems.in (see [[builders-bible]]), **@TheSwagWalaPM** as the YouTube and LinkedIn teaching brand (see [[the-swagwala-pm]]), and **PrabhupadaAI** as the AI spiritual Q&A application that carries the most personal meaning (see [[prabhupada-ai]]).

### Cohort-by-Cohort Growth Story

The growth trajectory from Cohort 1 to Cohort 8 tells the story of a company that compounds through community rather than capital. Cohort 1 launched in late 2023 with 52 students -- the first paying customers from a community that had been building trust through free YouTube content and the Art & Product Musings WhatsApp group (3,000+ active members, 100+ writing daily). The 100 participants in the first full MPM 2.0 cohort generated $150K+ in revenue with zero customer acquisition cost. Every student came through organic channels: YouTube discovery, LinkedIn posts, community referrals, and alumni word-of-mouth.

By Cohort 3 in 2024, enrollment had reached 80 students with an average experience of 10-16+ years. The quality of the cohort was remarkable -- these were not career switchers looking for their first PM role but senior operators looking to sharpen their edge. The NPS hit 91+, and the Instructor NPS reached 100 -- a perfect score that Shravan frames not as personal triumph but as proof that the model works. By Cohort 7 in January 2026, the community had grown to 400+ operators with 150+ career transitions. Applications had reached 350+ with a sub-10% acceptance rate, and 30% of the batch was already closed at soft launch before any active marketing.

The 8th cohort launches September 27, 2026, under the tagline "Built for Everyone." It represents a strategic evolution: Batch 7 was rebuilt as "Product x AI edition," weaving AI into every PM decision rather than adding it as a standalone topic. This mirrors Shravan's own professional evolution from PM educator to PM-who-builds educator, and reflects his conviction that "building in 2026 is not about who knows prompting -- it's about who can think clearly with AI as a co-builder."

### Revenue and Unit Economics

The financial story is unusual for edtech. Total revenue has crossed $1M through zero-to-one startups, with the first cohort alone generating $150K+ on zero marketing spend. The course price is $1,000 per participant, which functions as both a revenue model and a commitment device -- students who paid for the experience treat five-hour sessions differently than they would a free YouTube video. Shravan assigns his own time an explicit dollar value of $5,000 per hour and encourages students to do the same, not as bravado but as a prioritization framework.

The bootstrapped model means there are no investors to please, no growth targets imposed from outside, and no pressure to scale class sizes. This is by design. The hard cap of 25 students per group creates a natural tension with growth ambitions that the gurukul-inspired scaling model resolves. The unit of scale is a trained human, not a larger classroom. This is the antithesis of the edtech model Shravan saw firsthand at BYJU'S and Bhanzu, where massive scale, massive spend, and massive churn were the norm. ### Graduate Testimonials and the Alumni Network

The caliber of graduates and their testimonials tell a story that enrollment numbers alone cannot. **Varun Khanna** (Product Lead at Google, 12+ YOE) represents the most prestigious placement in the alumni network. **Isha Singal** (Sr. Technical PM at Amazon, 17+ YOE) demonstrates that even highly experienced operators find value in the program. **Kevin Thomas** (Senior AI PM at IBM, 5+ YOE) came to the cohort not for PM fundamentals but for storytelling and community building -- and later became a mentor. **Deepak Poddar** (Director Global PM at Mastercard, 20+ YOE) and **Puneet Mehra** (VP at Barclays, 21+ YOE) represent the most senior graduates, proving that the program scales up to director and VP level.

The graduate testimonial that moved Shravan most came from Batch 6: "This is not a product management program but a program deeply rooted in human values: resilience, persistence, and the principles of the Bhagavad Gita." He embraced this fully: "After 6 amazing batches, I've realized that this community was never only about learning PM -- it was always about something much bigger." The alumni-to-mentor pipeline is the mechanism by which the community compounds. Unnati was a Cohort 1 student who knew nothing about product management, cracked a PM role in four weeks, and now mentors incoming students. Each generation teaches the next, scaling the instructor's bandwidth without diluting the culture. The 400+ operator community functions as its own distribution channel, where alumni mentor, hire, and open doors for each other across companies including Meta, Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meesho, Swiggy, 1mg, Flipkart, Mastercard, Barclays, Oracle, Salesforce, Stripe, Boeing, and more.

### The Origin Story: From YouTube to Company

The Rethink Systems origin story traces back to July 2021, when Shravan launched [[the-swagwala-pm]] YouTube channel during his time as Principal PM at BYJU'S. The channel taught PM in Hinglish (Hindi-English mix), making content accessible to people who could not afford expensive certifications. It grew to 45K+ subscribers through consistent posting -- no viral moments, no paid promotion, just showing up daily since April 2020 on LinkedIn and July 2021 on YouTube. The WhatsApp community hit 1,700+ members. When enough community members asked for structured learning, the first cohort launched. The company was registered as Naum Infinitium Private Limited in February 2024, headquartered in Bengaluru. "Profitable from day 1" is the founding principle, and it has held through every cohort since.

Shravan names his early bosses and mentors who made this trajectory possible: Ravi Karthik taught him empathy and patience, Indraneel Bommisetty taught him creativity, and Lovleen Bhatia taught him how to face adversity. His gratitude always goes last to "my Mahadev, whose servant I am." ## Impact

Rethink Systems is the proof that the [[content-flywheel]] works at scale. Build in public, teach what you learn, attract a community, monetize through cohorts, reinvest into building more. $150K from a first cohort with zero CAC is the clearest evidence that community-first beats marketing-first for Shravan's model. The $1M+ revenue milestone, the 150+ career transitions, the 100 Instructor NPS, and the 400+ operator community are not separate achievements -- they are output metrics of a single system where community is the product, teaching is the growth engine, and content is the distribution layer. The company is not just a business -- it is the living thesis that everything compounds when the system is designed to feed itself.
