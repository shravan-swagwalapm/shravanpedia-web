> The parent company behind everything — community-led SaaS, education, and the middleware of the startup ecosystem.

## Context

**Rethink Systems** is the umbrella Shravan founded in 2024 after leaving Bhanzu. It is not one product — it is the platform that ties together the teaching, the building, and the community. Every sub-project — [[rethink-dashboard]], [[builders-bible]], [[the-swagwala-pm]], [[prabhupada-ai]] — lives under this roof. The thesis is community-first: build a community, then build products with and for that community. What makes Rethink distinctive in the crowded Indian edtech landscape is what it chose not to do. No venture capital. No paid ads. No race to millions of users. Instead, a deliberately intimate model where the community is the product, teaching is the growth engine, and content is the distribution layer.

## Details

### The Founding Story

The seeds of Rethink Systems were planted long before the company was registered. In April 2020, during the first COVID lockdown, Shravan began posting daily on LinkedIn about product management — not career advice in the conventional sense, but raw, first-principles thinking about what makes products work and what makes PMs effective. There was no content strategy, no editorial calendar, no plan. There was a PM at BYJU'S who felt that the knowledge locked inside companies should be free, and who had the discipline to post every single day without missing one.

By July 2021, the daily LinkedIn habit had built enough of an audience that launching a YouTube channel felt natural. [[the-swagwala-pm]] was born — teaching PM in Hinglish (Hindi-English mix), making content accessible to people who could not afford expensive certifications or did not have access to the English-language PM creator ecosystem that dominated the United States. The channel grew not through any single viral moment but through the compound interest of showing up daily. The WhatsApp community — "Art & Product Musings" — hit 3,000+ active members, with 100+ writing daily. It became a place where working PMs discussed real problems, not where aspirants practiced case studies.

The transition from content creator to company founder happened organically. Enough community members asked for structured learning that saying no felt like a disservice. Shravan ran a pilot cohort of 52 students in late 2023 — his first paying customers, drawn entirely from the community he had been building for three years. The experience was electric. Students who came expecting frameworks left saying they had learned to think differently. The feedback was so overwhelmingly positive that the decision to formalize was obvious.

**Naum Infinitium Private Limited** was incorporated in February 2024, headquartered in Bengaluru. The name is Sanskrit-Latin, meaning "infinite." The company was profitable from its first day of operations, and that principle has held through every cohort since. An earlier community called "All Things Product" predates or ran alongside the @TheSwagWalaPM branding, representing the first iteration of what would become Rethink's community model.

### What It Is

Rethink Systems operates as a community-led SaaS and education platform with two distinct arms. The first is **ed-tech** — education platforms, cohort programs, and free resources like the Builder's Bible. The second is **Talent-as-a-Service**, connecting trained professionals with startup opportunities. Shravan describes the company as "the middleware of the startup ecosystem," sitting between the talent and the companies that need it. This positioning is deliberate — rather than competing with job boards or recruiting firms, Rethink occupies the space where trained operators and growing companies meet, with the community as the trust layer that makes the connection valuable.

The company is bootstrapped and unfunded — growth has been entirely organic. There are no investors to please, no growth targets imposed from outside, and no pressure to scale class sizes. This is the antithesis of the edtech model Shravan saw firsthand at BYJU'S and Bhanzu, where massive scale, massive spend, and massive churn were the norm. Rethink proves that the opposite approach — intimate scale, zero marketing spend, and organic community — can cross $1M in revenue without a single rupee in advertising.

### Flagship Product: Mastering Product Management 2.0

The centerpiece offering is **Mastering Product Management 2.0**, an 8-week live cohort-based course focused on AI-first execution, design thinking, and real-world building. Each week includes co-building sessions, and the curriculum emphasizes first-principles product thinking, AI tool mastery, product decision-making, and practical execution. The course costs $1,000 per participant.

What makes the course distinctive is its refusal to teach PM as a collection of frameworks. The Bhagavad Gita sits alongside Marty Cagan in the curriculum. Case studies are deliberately unsolvable — the point is not the answer but the quality of the thinking. Sessions run five hours or longer, because Shravan believes that behavioral transformation requires sustained immersion, not bite-sized modules. The Week 1 mindset session introduces the [[three-superpowers]] — gratitude, unlearning, and compassion — before any product content begins. By Week 7, students discover that the entire curriculum was teaching [[systems-thinking]] in disguise.

The first cohort attracted 100 participants from Estonia to Croatia and generated **$150K+ in revenue with zero customer acquisition cost** — all from organic community. The 8th cohort is set to launch on September 27, 2026, under the tagline "Built for Everyone."

### Cohort Philosophy

Shravan holds a firm belief: "You cannot get quality education in any cohort greater than 25 people." This hard cap on cohort size drives a **gurukul-inspired scaling model** — develop 50 mentors who each train 20 educators. The strategy is to scale through people, not class size, preserving the intimacy that makes the education transformative. The model draws directly from the ancient Indian gurukul tradition, where a teacher's bandwidth was the constraint, and the solution was to multiply teachers rather than expand classrooms.

This creates a natural tension with growth ambitions that the model resolves elegantly. Every cohort produces not just graduates but potential mentors. The best students return to teach, creating a self-sustaining cycle where the community compounds in capability with each rotation. Unnati, a Cohort 1 student who knew nothing about product management, cracked a PM role in four weeks and now mentors incoming students. Kevin Thomas came for PM fundamentals and stayed to become a mentor. Each generation teaches the next, scaling the instructor's bandwidth without diluting the culture.

### Cohort-by-Cohort Growth Story

The growth trajectory from Cohort 1 to Cohort 8 tells the story of a company that compounds through community rather than capital. Cohort 1 launched in late 2023 with 52 students — the first paying customers from a community that had been building trust through free YouTube content and the Art & Product Musings WhatsApp group. The energy in that first cohort was something Shravan had not anticipated. Students were not just learning — they were forming bonds, helping each other outside class hours, staying up late to refine presentations together. The culture that would define Rethink was born in those first eight weeks.

The 100 participants in the first full MPM 2.0 cohort generated $150K+ in revenue with zero customer acquisition cost. Every student came through organic channels: YouTube discovery, LinkedIn posts, community referrals, and alumni word-of-mouth. By Cohort 3 in 2024, enrollment had reached 80 students with an average experience of 10-16+ years. The quality of the cohort was remarkable — these were not career switchers looking for their first PM role but senior operators looking to sharpen their edge. The NPS hit 91+, and the Instructor NPS reached 100 — a perfect score that validated the entire model.

By Cohort 7 in January 2026, the community had grown to 400+ operators with 150+ career transitions. Applications had reached 350+ with a sub-10% acceptance rate, and 30% of the batch was already closed at soft launch before any active marketing. The selectivity was not gatekeeping — it was quality control. Shravan discovered early that the density of talent in the room matters as much as what the instructor teaches. When every person in a cohort of 25 is a serious operator, the peer learning multiplies the value of every session.

The 8th cohort launches September 27, 2026, under the tagline "Built for Everyone." It represents a strategic evolution: Batch 7 was rebuilt as "Product x AI edition," weaving AI into every PM decision rather than adding it as a standalone topic. This mirrors Shravan's own professional evolution from PM educator to PM-who-builds educator, and reflects his conviction that "building in 2026 is not about who knows prompting — it's about who can think clearly with AI as a co-builder."

### Revenue and Unit Economics

The financial story is unusual for edtech. Total revenue has crossed $1M through zero-to-one startups, with the first cohort alone generating $150K+ on zero marketing spend. The course price is $1,000 per participant, which functions as both a revenue model and a commitment device — students who paid for the experience treat five-hour sessions differently than they would a free YouTube video. Shravan assigns his own time an explicit dollar value of $5,000 per hour and encourages students to do the same, not as bravado but as a prioritization framework.

The bootstrapped model means there are no investors to please, no growth targets imposed from outside, and no pressure to scale class sizes. This is by design. The hard cap of 25 students per group creates a natural tension with growth ambitions that the gurukul-inspired scaling model resolves. The unit of scale is a trained human, not a larger classroom. ### The Community as a Living Organism

What distinguishes Rethink from other edtech companies is that the community is not a feature of the product — it is the product. The multi-day Bangalore retreats achieve NPS of 80+, judged by industry leaders. Guest masters include SVPs and CPOs from Zee, Adobe, Amazon, LaunchDarkly, and Cashfree. The global reach spans 15+ countries with 20% international students — from Estonia to Croatia to Singapore to the United States.

Shravan describes the dynamic: "Today, this large community of operators across the world has become family — not like family, but truly family." This is not marketing copy. It is visible in the behavior: alumni who spontaneously congratulate incoming students, mentors who take calls at midnight for a student in a different time zone, graduates who fly to Bangalore for retreats they are not required to attend. The community energy is self-sustaining because the selection process ensures that every person who enters brings something worth sharing, and the culture ensures that sharing is the default behavior.

## Impact

Rethink Systems is the proof that the [[content-flywheel]] works at scale. Build in public, teach what you learn, attract a community, monetize through cohorts, reinvest into building more. $150K from a first cohort with zero CAC is the clearest evidence that community-first beats marketing-first for Shravan's model. The $1M+ revenue milestone, the 150+ career transitions, the 100 Instructor NPS, and the 400+ operator community are not separate achievements — they are output metrics of a single system where community is the product, teaching is the growth engine, and content is the distribution layer.

What Rethink proves to the broader edtech world is that scale and intimacy are not in opposition. You do not have to choose between reaching millions and changing lives. The gurukul model — intimate cohorts, trained mentors, organic growth — achieves both by scaling through people rather than through classrooms. The company is not just a business — it is the living thesis that everything compounds when the system is designed to feed itself. And at its heart, it is the expression of a builder who learned from the Bhagavad Gita that when you serve others without attachment to outcomes, the outcomes exceed anything you could have planned.
