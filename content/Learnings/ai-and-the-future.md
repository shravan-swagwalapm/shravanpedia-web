> "AI is your side brain, not your replacement. But first principles and clarity of thought still win."

## Context

Shravan's AI stance is distinctive: he is neither a doomer nor a hype merchant. He is a builder who uses AI every day -- Claude Code as his CTO, [[prabhupada-and-iskcon]] as a shipping RAG product, vibe coding courses for students -- while simultaneously insisting that AI without taste, empathy, and first-principles thinking is worthless. He watches the layoff numbers closely, not to scare people, but to wake them up. His core message since 2025: you are no longer competing with people, you are competing with people who wield AI better than you.

## Details

### The Layoff Wake-Up Call

Shravan tracks and publishes tech layoff numbers with alarm-clock energy. Across multiple posts from 2025 to 2026, he cites the same escalating figures: Amazon, Google, Microsoft, and others have now crossed 200,000+ layoffs. His framing is consistent and urgent -- these layoffs did not happen because people failed, but because they assumed AI was the future when in reality it was already rewriting the present.

The implication he draws is sharp: "You're no longer competing with just people. You're competing with PMs and engineers who wield AI like a cheat code and can ship in half the time at a fraction of the cost." The antidote he prescribes is equally direct -- stop scrolling reels and start sharpening your AI skills.

### AI as Second Brain, Not Replacement

This is the core thesis, repeated in [[rethink-systems]] Batch 7 and beyond: PMs will not be replaced by AI, but they will be replaced by PMs who treat AI like a capability, not a checkbox. When a student called him after a layoff and asked "Bhaiya, AI everywhere hai. Main late toh nahi ho gayi?" his answer crystallized the philosophy: "AI is your side brain, not your replacement. But first principles and clarity of thought still win."

He rebuilt the Rethink Systems curriculum around this conviction. The program did not simply add AI as a topic -- it wove AI into every PM decision: discovery, execution, analytics, storytelling, experiments, delivery. The distinction matters because it treats AI as a way of working, not a subject to study.

### Building With AI (Not Just Talking About It)

Shravan practices what he preaches across multiple production projects. **PrabhupadaAI** is a RAG-based web app that lets users ask questions answered with Srila Prabhupada's teachings, built with Claude Code, FastAPI, and deployed on Railway. His **Claude Code as CTO** workflow treats Claude Opus as a senior technical co-founder through a CLAUDE.md configuration that enforces engineering judgment, not just code generation. He created free **Vibe Coding courses** teaching students to build products with AI assistance, plus a separate **AI Agents for Everyone** course shared on LinkedIn. And the **Ask SwagwalaPM GPT** is a custom GPT built on everything he has learned, written, and shared -- framed as "me, with you 24/7."

### The Free AI MBA

In January 2026, he published a nine-month self-study plan for becoming an AI PM, built entirely from free resources. Months 1-2 cover LLM foundations through Andrej Karpathy's deep dives, 3Blue1Brown, and the a16z AI Canon. Months 3-4 focus on RAG and context engineering via NVIDIA explainers and Pinecone guides. Months 5-6 tackle prompt engineering and agents through Anthropic tutorials, n8n agent guides, and Microsoft's AI Agents for Beginners. Months 7-8 address AI evals and quality with Hamel Husain's FAQ and Lenny's PM Guide to Evals. Month 9 brings it together with AI UX and shipping through Karpathy's "How I Use LLMs," Shape of AI patterns, and Agentic UX patterns.

### People He Respects in the AI Space

The people Shravan references most reveal his intellectual influences. **Andrej Karpathy** appears repeatedly as the definitive teacher for LLM foundations. **Dario Amodei**, Anthropic's CEO, gets the strongest endorsement -- Shravan recommends the five-hour Lex Fridman interview as a "masterclass." **Ilya Sutskever's** "Age of Scaling is Over" talk is cited as foundational. **Fei-Fei Li** is called the "Godmother of AI on World Models." Among product leaders, **Kevin Weil** (OpenAI's CPO) resonates with his emphasis on eval-writing as the most important PM skill, **Nick Turley** (Head of ChatGPT) for building the fastest-growing product in history, **Shreyas Doshi** (Anthropic advisor) for high agency and the LNO framework, and **Lenny Rachitsky** as "the OG of product content" whose AI PM resources he curates and credits.

### The Contrarian Edge

His take on AI PM hiring cuts against the grain: "Building in 2026 is not about who knows prompting. It's about who can think clearly with AI as a co-builder." He pushes back on the notion that AI skills are about tools, insisting instead that AI intuition is freely available -- but without empathy, curiosity, and the ability to lead by demonstrating action, no AI can take away the PM role from a human who possesses those qualities.

He also identifies a massive compensation arbitrage: AI PMs at OpenAI and Anthropic are pulling $500K+ packages while most PMs still treat AI as optional. His bet is that the gap between AI-literate and AI-ignorant product people will only widen.

## Impact

AI has reshaped everything Shravan builds and teaches. His program evolved from "Mastering Product Management" to "Product x AI Evolution Edition." His own projects are AI-native -- PrabhupadaAI, Claude Code workflows, automated pipelines. He invested in 20+ startups across 7+ countries, many in the AI space. But the deepest impact is philosophical: AI validated his belief that generalists who think in systems will outlast specialists who only know one tool. The future belongs to people who can think, not just prompt.
