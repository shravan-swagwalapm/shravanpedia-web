> "You don't need a lot of frameworks to become a better PM, you just have to be a better human to become a better PM."

## Context

Product management is Shravan's core professional identity -- 9+ years as a product leader, 1,983 LinkedIn posts on the topic, a YouTube channel ([[the-swagwala-pm]]), and a full education company ([[rethink-systems]]) built around teaching it. His PM philosophy is not academic. It was forged through real failure, real hiring conversations, and real students cracking roles. This is his most-posted-about topic by a wide margin.

## Details

### The Founder Mindset

The single strongest conviction across all of Shravan's PM posts is that **a PM should think and act like a founder**. He tells a defining story repeatedly -- posted five or more times between 2022 and 2026 -- about failing a product sense interview, then spending 15 hours doing deep research, writing a comprehensive PRD, and winning the role with a 100% salary hike. The founder who hired him said: "Lakde, maine zindagi main ek cheez seekhi hai -- jo log apne passion ke liye sab kuch peeche chod dete hai, woh log zindagi main zaroor kuch acha karte hai."

The lesson he extracts every time he tells it: "I didn't hire you because of your PRD. I looked at your attempt to genuinely solve a problem comprehensively in a really pressured scenario." PMs must take initiative as if the product is theirs. Passion and persistence beat polish. This story functions as his origin myth because it encodes his deepest belief about what makes a PM exceptional.

### PM Anti-Patterns

Shravan wrote a detailed taxonomy of why colleagues end up hating PMs, and it reads like a mirror held up to the profession. **The Know-it-all** operates with an inflated level of competence, giving instructions instead of suggestions -- when the better move is "How did you pick X over Y?" rather than "Y is better, let's change it." **The 30K Feet PM** takes vague requests and throws them across the fence, when the job is to create clarity on what to build and why. **The Indecisive PM** cannot stop adding scope -- "Let's add one more feature!" -- and puts the creep in scope creep. **The Visionary PM** hoards credit and deflects blame, embodying the principle that "vision without execution is mere hallucination." **The CEO/Deadline Tyrant** does not code or design but is supremely confident about how long things take, ignoring that estimates are hard and the devil is in the details.

His antidote cuts across all five: "Don't act like the CEO. Make your invisible work visible. Share your level of conviction. Be generous with credit -- your team did more than you think, and you did less than you realize."

### Compassion as Product Thinking

One of Shravan's most distinctive takes is that product sense comes from **compassion, not frameworks**. He tells a story about rescuing an injured stray dog that led him to build a curated pet adoption marketplace with the animal husbandry department. He calls this the "Compassion Funnel": compassion outward toward the world leads to curiosity about how to solve problems at scale, which leads to passion for testing multiple hypotheses and finding what works, which leads to critical thinking -- because if your core problem hypothesis is wrong, the solution cannot help anyone.

"It's the art of solving problems at scale but also ensuring it builds a happiness layer for the user." This framing is not sentimental decoration. It is a genuine alternative to the frameworks-first approach that dominates PM education.

### AI as a Second Brain, Not a Replacement

His 2025-2026 evolution reframes AI's role for product managers. "Building in 2026 is not about who knows prompting. It's about who can think clearly with AI as a co-builder." He positions AI as a "side brain" -- first principles and clarity of thought still win. His program, MPM 2.0, weaves AI into every PM decision: discovery, execution, analytics, storytelling. The overarching conviction: "Great PMs don't just ship features, they ship trust."

### Teaching as Mission

He started [[the-swagwala-pm]] YouTube channel in 2021, and the metric that matters to him is not subscriber count but impact: 200+ PMs transitioned into roles free of cost, 500+ through Rethink Systems. He curates structured 5-week playlists and recommends books -- always including the Bhagavad Gita alongside *Inspired* by Marty Cagan, never as a separate category.

His sign-off is always "Yours, Shravan Bhaiya," and the positioning is deliberate. Not professor, not guru. Bhaiya -- older brother. Accessible, personal, relational. The way an older brother teaches is different from how a professor lectures: it is patient, it is honest, and it assumes you will eventually get it.

### The Rethink Systems Model

[[rethink-systems]] was built after 9 years in product leadership. Bootstrapped to 6+ figures annually. No VC, no board meetings. 400+ operators across 10+ countries. NPS of 92+ across every cohort. His framing: "This is not a program but the path to the best versions of yourself."

His students from Batch 6 said something that clearly moved him deeply: "Shravan, you have duped us into learning human values instead of product management." He took it as the highest possible compliment, and his response captures the entire philosophy: "This community was never only about learning PM -- it was about unlearning mistakes from the past, becoming a better human every day, forgiving yourself, and building a brighter future."

### Core Book Recommendations

He posts a curated reading list regularly, and the consistent picks define his professional canon: *Inspired* and *Empowered* by Marty Cagan, *Continuous Discovery Habits* by Teresa Torres, *High Output Management* by Andy Grove, *The Lean Product Playbook* by Dan Olsen, *Radical Candor* by Kim Scott, and *Team of Rivals* -- which he calls "the best book on how to influence without authority." The Bhagavad Gita as It Is by Lord Krishna is always listed alongside the PM books, never in a separate spiritual category, because for Shravan the two domains are not separate.

## Impact

PM is not just what Shravan does -- it is the lens through which he sees every problem. His philosophy is distinctly humanistic in a field that trends toward frameworks and metrics. He teaches product sense through compassion, positions the Gita alongside Marty Cagan, and measures success in humans helped rather than revenue generated. The founder mindset story is his origin myth -- told and retold because it encodes his deepest belief: passion and genuine problem-solving outweigh credentials every time.
