## Context

Career advice is Shravan's most consistent LinkedIn topic -- 729 posts and counting. He has been writing about PM transitions since 2021, when he started [[the-swagwala-pm]] YouTube channel. More than 200 PMs have transitioned into full-time roles using his free content alone. This is not theoretical advice. It is battle-tested, born from his own messy career path through four job changes, an IIT degree he used to lie about, and a health crisis that nearly killed him mid-founder-journey.

## Details

### On Layoffs and the AI Reckoning

The single most repeated theme across Shravan's career posts: mass layoffs are not temporary. Amazon, Google, Microsoft, and others have now crossed 200,000+ layoffs in 2025-2026. His framing is consistent -- these layoffs did not happen because people failed, but because they assumed AI was the future when in reality it was already rewriting the present. He has been saying this since 2024 and continues to amplify the message: "Stop scrolling reels and start sharpening your AI skills."

His deeper take: AI is your side brain, not your replacement. "Building in 2026 is not about who knows prompting. It's about who can think clearly with AI as a co-builder." First principles and clarity of thought still win. The layoff numbers are not doom -- they are an alarm clock for anyone still treating AI as optional.

### On Hiring -- Founder Material Over Credentials

Shravan's most-shared career story is his own origin myth. He bombed an SPM interview, asked for a day to redeem himself, spent 15 hours writing a comprehensive PRD with primary and secondary research, and the founder gave him a 100% hike. The founder's words stayed with him: "Lakde, maine zindagi main ek cheez seekhi hai -- jo log apne passion ke liye sab kuch peeche chod dete hai, woh log zindagi main zaroor kuch acha karte hai."

The lesson he extracts: "I didn't hire you because of your PRD. What I looked at was your attempt to genuinely solve a problem comprehensively in a really pressured scenario." A PM should be founder material -- "not the founder, but definitely founder material who takes initiative as if it's his/her product." This is not about entrepreneurship. It is about ownership, the willingness to treat someone else's product as if your name were on the door.

### On Interviewing -- Compassion Over Gatekeeping

When Shravan interviews, he looks for potential, not polish. He tells a story about interviewing for a friend's startup: the candidate went completely blank, profusely sweating on Google Meet. Instead of moving on, Shravan told him, "Dude, think of it as two cool people just trying to solve a problem." The candidate solved it brilliantly. "90% of all success is encouragement. Most successful PMs are not successful because of their skills but their ability to inspire others to do well and give chances when no one does."

That candidate is now a GPM who built a team of passionate people -- "not because they are from IIT, IIMs but because they have the ability to build something remarkable." The story encodes a belief that runs through all of Shravan's career advice: the interview process should discover ability, not filter for confidence.

### On PM Anti-Patterns

He catalogued the five PM types people hate: the Know-it-all, the 30K Feet PM, the Indecisive PM, the Visionary PM, and the Deadline Tyrant. His honest take: "These characters are not just caricatures. They exist inside each of us." The fix is not frameworks but self-awareness: "Don't act like the CEO. Be generous with credit -- your team did more than you think, and you did less than you realize."

### On Resumes and Prep

His resume advice is characteristically blunt: "Impress with facts, not with big words that make you look important (because they don't)." A bad summary reads "visionary product management executive." A good one reads "5 years building consumer marketplace products for Swiggy, Flipkart. Generated 200M revenue." Specificity is credibility.

He recommends YouTube over certifications for PM prep: "YouTube has led to more PM offers than any certification has." He curates PM prep playlists featuring Dan Olsen, Lenny Rachitsky, and Exponent, because structured free content beats expensive courses. His own 5-week free framework on the Swag Wala PM channel has been the primary transition tool for 50+ PMs who credited it directly.

### On Storytelling as the Core PM Skill

"When I meet people and am asked what I do for a living, my response is 'Product management' but the truth is 'I am a storyteller or am trying to be one.'" Great PMs tell stories to engineers, to customers, to stakeholders. "Move away from the habit of writing boring user stories. Tell a good story, and you'll end up with a passionate team who works on products your customers love." Storytelling is not a soft skill in Shravan's framework -- it is the core mechanism through which PMs create alignment, and alignment is the only thing that ships products.

### On Health and Founder Life

In 2025, at 105 kg, Shravan fainted. His doctor said: "If you continue like this, your body will die before your startup does." He lost 23 kg through boring consistency -- OMAD, 30,000 steps a day, waking at 5:30 AM, chanting. The career lesson he draws from the health journey is direct: "You cannot be a 1% operator at work if you're careless with life. How you do anything is how you do everything." A broken body produces broken judgment, and broken judgment produces broken products.

### On Rethink Systems as the Vehicle

Seven cohorts of Mastering Product Management. 400+ operators. 150+ career transitions. Instructor NPS of 100 across all cohorts. "This is truly unprecedented in the product management ecosystem and puts us on the path to becoming the best damn operator program in India, and then the planet." [[rethink-systems]] is not just a business -- it is the proof that career transformation at scale works when you combine rigorous selection, human values, and relentless execution.

## Impact

This body of work -- the YouTube channel, the LinkedIn posts, the Rethink cohorts -- is the manifestation of what Shravan found in the Bhagavad Gita in 2020: "Compassion was the key to happiness. When you help others without expectation, bliss manifests." Career content is the vehicle, but the real mission is making people believe they can do hard things. The first time someone told him "Bhaiya meri job lag gaya," he was happy without bounds for the first time in 28 years. That moment is what all 729 posts are about.
