> Founder, product manager, teacher, angel investor, and builder — the person this wiki exists for.

## Context

**Shravan Tickoo** (born February 28, 1993) is an Indian product manager, entrepreneur, educator, and angel investor who has built one of the largest product management communities in the world from a tier-2 city in northern India. He is the founder and CEO of [[rethink-systems]], a community-led SaaS and education company that has crossed $1 million in revenue while remaining bootstrapped and profitable from its first day. He is the creator of [[the-swagwala-pm]], a YouTube channel with over 45,000 subscribers that teaches product management and AI-assisted development in Hinglish — the Hindi-English mix that defines India's tech conversation. He is the author of the [[builders-bible]], a 700+ page free book on engineering and AI. And he is an active angel investor with a portfolio of more than 20 startups across India, the United States, Canada, Pakistan, and Bangladesh.

As of April 2026, Tickoo has mentored over 10,000 product managers, placed 300+ into PM roles, and built a LinkedIn following of 199,000 with over 190 million impressions per year. Favikon ranked him the best Product Management creator in India and third worldwide. The Product Folks named him one of India's top 100 product managers. He has lectured at IITs, IIMs, ISB, Masters' Union, and NTU Singapore, and has appeared on podcasts including The Growth Podcast with Aakash Gupta and The One Percent Project.

What makes Tickoo's story unusual is not the scale of his reach but where it started. He grew up in Jammu, a city that does not appear on any startup map. He studied Metallurgy at IIT Roorkee — not Computer Science, not IIT Bombay. He is entirely self-taught in product management. And in 2025, he began shipping production software using AI tools, despite having no formal engineering training. The trajectory he describes — "app builder to AI systems builder" — is the one he teaches others to follow, and the proof that it works is in his own story.

His spiritual life as an ISKCON devotee runs through everything he builds. The teachings of Srila Prabhupada are not a side interest or a cultural reference but the foundational lens through which he evaluates every decision — from how he prices his courses to why he built [[prabhupada-ai]], the project he describes as carrying the deepest personal meaning. See [[prabhupada-and-iskcon]] and [[nishkama-karma-and-building]] for the full spiritual framework.

## Details

### Early life and the boy from Jammu

Shravan Tickoo grew up in Jammu, a tier-2 city in the foothills of the Himalayas, far from the startup corridors of Bangalore and Mumbai. From his earliest years, he displayed the kind of relentless curiosity that made his family simultaneously proud and worried. In 6th grade, visiting his Mamaji in Udhampur, an old tenant lady called him "Ravan" because she could not say "Shravan" — and because, she said, "this boy breaks everything and is so curious he uproots the plants." His great-grandmother joked he had "come to kill me" because he never stopped asking questions.

By 8th grade, when the family acquired their first computer, Shravan was reading about Egyptian mythology, music, religion, and the philosophy of mathematics — "basically anything besides my academics." In 10th grade, while his classmates focused on board exam preparation, he was reading about Hilbert's problems and the design of mathematical systems. Relatives stopped inviting him to their homes, worried his unconventional interests would be a bad influence on their children. This pattern — deep curiosity directed toward whatever fascinated him most, regardless of whether it was expected or practical — became the origin story he would return to again and again in his teaching career. The boy who was "too curious" for his family became the teacher who tells students that curiosity is the only unfakeable competitive advantage.

His childhood was also shaped by simulation games. While other kids played action titles, Shravan was drawn to Age of Empires and strategy games that, as he puts it, "rewarded strategy over speed." He credits these games with developing the instincts that would later define his approach to product management: strategic thinking, resource allocation, reading complex systems, and the understanding that "playing the game, not winning it, is the point." The parallels between a well-designed simulation game and a well-designed product — both are systems where the user's experience emerges from invisible rules — became a reference point he still uses in his cohort teaching.

### Education: IIT Roorkee and the Young India Fellowship

Tickoo attended **IIT Roorkee** from 2011 to 2015, studying Metallurgical Engineering in the top 5% of his batch. Throughout college, he volunteered with the National Service Scheme (NSS), teaching mathematics to underprivileged students — the earliest evidence of a teaching instinct that predates his professional career by a full decade. At ages 18 to 22, before he had any professional identity, before the YouTube channel or the LinkedIn following or the cohort programs, the impulse to teach was already the constant.

He was subsequently selected as a **Young India Fellow** through the Ashoka University and University of Pennsylvania collaboration — a program with a less than 2% acceptance rate, designed for multidisciplinary thinkers. The fellowship reinforced the same pattern visible from childhood: the generalist curiosity that made relatives worry about him became, in the right institutional context, a validated strength. The fellowship exposed him to cross-disciplinary thinking — the same kind of thinking that would later make his PM teaching distinctive, drawing from the Bhagavad Gita, simulation games, mathematics, and Korean kindness in the same breath.

### The Arizona app: first venture

Before entering the corporate world, Tickoo built a location-tracking app similar to Waze that went viral in Arizona, United States — without any marketing spend. This was approximately 2014-2015, before his formal PM career had begun. The app spread organically in a foreign market, with zero promotion, validating an instinct that would define everything that followed: build something genuinely useful, and the audience finds you. This principle — value creates its own distribution — became the foundation of what he later formalized as the [[content-flywheel]]. The Arizona app is the earliest proof that the flywheel works, and he tells the story in every cohort as evidence that distribution is not a problem you solve after building; it is a property of building well.

### Career: from Flipkart to founding

Tickoo's corporate career spans nine years across six companies, each role adding a layer to the builder-teacher identity that would culminate in Rethink Systems.

His journey began at **Flipkart** as a Graduate Trainee (2015-2016), where he experienced India's most ambitious e-commerce operation from the inside. He moved to **BlackBuck** (2016-2017) for his first dedicated product role, where he won Employee of the Year. It was at BlackBuck that a senior PM pulled him aside over lunch and delivered the three lessons that shaped his entire management philosophy: compassion over control, autonomy breeds excellence, and focus only on what you can change. He retold this story at least four times across 2021-2023, always with the same gratitude: "He may not remember me, but he was a mentor to me." See [[personal-stories]] for the full account.

He then moved through **Magicbricks** (2017-2018), **Edureka** as Senior Product Manager (2018-2020) where he won Star Performer, **BYJU'S** as Principal Product Manager (2020-2022), and **Bhanzu** as Director of Product Management (2022-2023). He is entirely self-taught in product management — no certifications, no MBA, no formal PM training. Everything he knows, he learned by doing, reading voraciously, and reflecting publicly.

In 2024, after nine years as a product operator, he founded [[rethink-systems]], registered as Naum Infinitium Private Limited. What started as a WhatsApp group called "Art & Product Musings" — which grew to 3,000+ active members with 100+ writing daily — became a cohort-based education company. The first cohort generated over $150,000 in revenue with zero customer acquisition cost. By April 2026, the company had crossed $1 million in revenue, mentored 10,000+ PMs, produced 150+ career transitions, and was launching its 8th cohort in September 2026. It has been profitable from day one, and Tickoo frames that as a deliberate choice: "Profitable from day 1" is not a brag but a founding principle. See [[rethink-systems]] for the full company story.

### Teaching: from YouTube to institution

Tickoo launched [[the-swagwala-pm]] YouTube channel in July 2021, during his time as Principal PM at BYJU'S. The name itself is a statement: a PM who actually ships, with the confidence ("swag") that comes from building, not just managing. The channel teaches in Hinglish — the natural language of India's tech community — making concepts accessible to students who cannot afford expensive English-language certifications. It has grown to 45,000+ subscribers, with a comprehensive PM curriculum spanning 15+ live session series.

His teaching has evolved through three distinct phases. Phase 1 (2021-2023) was pure PM education: breaking into PM, fundamentals, case studies. Phase 2 (2024) added the Rethink Systems cohort funnel, where the channel became the top of the funnel for the $1,000/seat "Mastering PM 2.0" course. Phase 3 (2025-present) is the AI-builder pivot: Claude Code workshops, the Builder's Bible, and teaching non-developers to ship production software. The channel's evolution mirrors Tickoo's own: PM educator to PM-who-builds educator to AI-builder educator.

Beyond YouTube, his teaching footprint spans India's most prestigious institutions. He has lectured at **IITs, IIMs, IIM Lucknow** (invited speaker at "Inflection Point 4.0"), **ISB, Masters' Union** (as a Visiting Master for "Breaking into Product Management"), and **NTU Singapore**. He has appeared on **The One Percent Project** (Episode 31, September 2021: "How to Build and Scale Products for Unicorns") and **The Growth Podcast with Aakash Gupta** (October 2024: "The Product Management Job Search in 2024: How to Win"). He was recognized by **The Product Folks** as one of their "100 PMs" — a peer-nominated list of top product leaders in India.

His educational approach is what makes his teaching distinctive. He weaves Bhagavad Gita wisdom into modern product execution. He opens every cohort with the [[three-superpowers]] — Attitude of Gratitude, Ability to Unlearn, and Power of Compassion — before teaching any PM frameworks. He uses the [[commanders-intent]] military framework to teach why plans fail but intent endures. His case studies are deliberately unsolvable, because the point is not the answer but the ability to navigate ambiguity. His Batch 6 graduation produced the statement that moved him most: "This is not a product management program but a program deeply rooted in human values: resilience, persistence, and the principles of the Bhagavad Gita." See [[teaching-and-mentoring]] and [[mpm-cohort-pedagogy]] for the full methodology.

### The builder identity

What distinguishes Tickoo from a traditional PM educator is that he builds production software himself. Not trained as a developer, he ships code using AI-assisted development tools — primarily Claude Code, which he treats as a CTO-level collaborator rather than an autocomplete tool. This approach, documented in [[claude-code-as-cto]], forms the basis of his teaching philosophy: that product managers should be able to build, not just manage.

He typically maintains five to eight active projects simultaneously. His current portfolio includes [[prabhupada-ai]] (AI spiritual Q&A grounded in Prabhupada's teachings), [[predict-my-layoff]] (Career Risk Scanner that went viral on LinkedIn), the [[builders-bible]] (700+ page free engineering book), [[rethink-dashboard]] (ed-tech platform with student and admin views), and several others. Each project feeds the [[content-flywheel]] — building creates teaching material, teaching attracts community, community funds the next build.

His building philosophy is captured in three rules: "Do, then report" (never narrate intent), "Only what was asked" (no bonus features), and "Prove it" (not done until there is evidence). He uses Obsidian as his persistent knowledge store across all sessions, and his design references include Linear and Stripe as aesthetic benchmarks. See [[building-philosophy]] and [[design-philosophy]] for the complete frameworks.

### Angel investing

Tickoo has been an active angel investor since December 2020, with a portfolio spanning more than 20 startups across India, the United States, Canada, Pakistan, and Bangladesh. His investments span healthtech, edtech, SaaS, and consumer tech, with named investments including Foreign Admits, Airtribe, Growrk, Maqsad, Go Paani, MDplus, Thekabook, and Cur8. His investment philosophy is founder-first and impact-weighted — he looks for the same behavioral pattern he admires in the 100% hike story: founders who will spend 15 hours on a problem because they cannot help themselves, not because someone asked them to. See [[angel-investing]] for the full portfolio and philosophy.

### The spiritual foundation

In 2020, while in the grip of years of accumulated professional pressure, Tickoo read the **Bhagavad Gita As It Is** by A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada. The effect was not gradual — it was a turning point. He stopped caring about credentials and started focusing entirely on how he could help others. The Gita's teaching on nishkama karma — action without attachment to outcomes — became his operating principle. "I didn't care now if I was from IIT R or B. The only thing I cared about was how I can help the next person better."

Five years later, in 2025, a health wake-up call deepened the spiritual practice. He began waking at 5:30 AM, chanting, sitting quietly. He walked 30,000 steps a day for sixty days straight. He transformed his body from 105 kg to 82 kg. "The real transformation wasn't physical. I could run again. Play again. Be present in my own life. At 32, I felt alive — properly alive."

Today, the spiritual practice is the infrastructure beneath everything visible. The morning chanting produces the equanimity he brings to five-hour teaching sessions. The Gita's teachings on dharma inform why he caps cohorts at 25 students when scaling to 200 would be more profitable. The principle of seva (service) explains why the @TheSwagWalaPM channel started with free content and why the Builder's Bible is free forever. He built [[prabhupada-ai]] — a full RAG application with voice synthesis — because making Prabhupada's teachings accessible through technology felt like the most meaningful thing he could build. See [[prabhupada-and-iskcon]], [[the-spiritual-foundation]], and [[gita-principles-in-product-management]] for the complete spiritual framework.

### The health transformation (2025)

In April 2025, Shravan's doctor delivered a wake-up call: "If you continue like this, your body will die before your startup does." Rethink Systems was growing fast — 14 to 16 hour days of teaching, traveling, and creating content. His weight had crossed 105 kg. He fainted. A full health check revealed numbers that demanded immediate change.

What followed was one of the most disciplined transformations in his story. Starting June 2025, he walked 7,000 steps daily. Then 10,000. Then 20,000. Then 30,000 steps a day for sixty days straight. He shifted to OMAD (one meal a day), protein-heavy. He started waking at 5:30 AM, chanting, sitting quietly — the spiritual practice and the physical discipline fusing into a single morning routine. Three months later: 82 kg, approximately 16% body fat, clean reports across every metric.

He frames the transformation not as a fitness achievement but as a philosophical realization: "You cannot be a 1% operator at work if you're careless with life. How you do anything is how you do everything." The health crisis and its resolution are inseparable from his teaching. When he tells cohort students about discipline, consistency, and playing long games, the proof is in the 30,000 daily steps. When he teaches the Gita's prescription for regulated habits (BG 6.16-17), the proof is in the 5:30 AM chanting that replaced the 2 AM coding sessions. See [[life-philosophy]] for the complete philosophical framework.

### Honors and recognition

Tickoo has received recognition across multiple dimensions of his career. In 2024, **Favikon** ranked him the best Product Management creator in India and third worldwide on LinkedIn — a ranking he framed not as personal triumph but community proof: "Coming from a tier-2 city like Jammu, I never thought my voice would resonate with so many people. If a boy from Jammu can do this, we can achieve anything."

His other honors include the **Uber Bug Bounty** ($3,000 reward for identifying a data vulnerability — early evidence of his builder instinct before he had the PM title), the **K Start Fellowship**, **Star Performer at Edureka**, **Employee of the Year at BlackBuck**, and recognition by **The Product Folks** as one of India's top 100 product managers (peer-nominated). He has spoken at BizTalk at IIM Lucknow, and his LinkedIn presence — 199,000+ followers, 190 million+ impressions per year — has made him one of the most visible PM voices in the world.

## Impact

Shravan Tickoo is the central node of this wiki. Every project, person, inspiration, learning, and idea in Shravanpedia connects back to his journey as a builder-teacher. The career arc from a curious boy in Jammu to IIT Roorkee to Flipkart graduate trainee to founding a million-dollar education company — and the teaching that ran parallel to every single role — is the throughline.

What makes his story compelling is not the scale of the achievements but the consistency of the principles. The boy who taught math to underprivileged students through NSS is the same person who caps his cohort at 25 because quality education cannot happen at scale. The traveler saved by a Korean stranger's kindness is the same person who built the Compassion Funnel as a PM framework. The Gita reader who stopped caring about credentials in 2020 is the same person who signs every post "Yours, Shravan Bhaiya" — not as branding, but as an expression of the elder-brother relationship he carries with his community.

His story, as he frames it, is proof of a simple thesis: "If a boy from Jammu can do this, we can achieve anything."
