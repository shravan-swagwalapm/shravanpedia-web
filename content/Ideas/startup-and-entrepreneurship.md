> "A PM should be founder material — not the founder, but definitely founder material who takes initiative as if it's his/her product."

## Context

Across 532 posts about startups, founding, and entrepreneurship, a clear philosophy emerges. But Shravan is not a commentator — he is in the arena. [[rethink-systems]] is his first company, built with no safety net, no off switch, and no playbook. His startup views are forged from living it: the 14-16 hour days, the health crisis, the cohort-by-cohort grind of building an education company that compounds through community, not capital.

## Details

### Founder Mentality as the Core Belief

The single most repeated idea across Shravan's startup posts is that the best employees think like founders. "A founder gets paid like a founder." When the startup founder who hired him saw that Shravan had spent 15 hours writing a PRD after bombing an interview, he did not care about the document — he cared about the behavior. "This happens to me daily in my startup and you acted as if you are the founder in place of me and I don't let go of people who are founder material."

This idea runs through everything he teaches: "As a PM, acting like a founder is paramount, and trust me then your value is far more significant than any compensation you get. Passion and persistence is the key."

### Building Rethink Systems — The Bootstrap Story

Rethink Systems was never venture-funded. It grew cohort by cohort. What started as a YouTube channel (Swag Wala PM) in 2021 became a first cohort in late 2023 with 52 students. By Cohort 3 in 2024, enrollment reached 80 students with 10-16+ years average experience, a 91+ NPS, and an Instructor NPS of 100. By Cohort 7 in January 2026, the community had grown to 400+ operators with 150+ career transitions, and 350+ applications came in with 30% of the batch already closed at soft launch. Alumni now work at Meta, Microsoft, Meesho, Tata 1mg, and more.

"This is truly unprecedented in the product management ecosystem and puts us on the path to becoming the best damn operator program in India, and then the planet." The acceptance rate is under 10%. Growth is entirely organic — content, referrals, results.

### The Founder's Health Tax

Shravan's most vulnerable startup post came in 2025: "It was April 2025, and my doctor said: 'If you continue like this, your body will die before your startup does.'" His weight had crossed 105 kg. Cholesterol, triglycerides, everything in red. "This was my first time building something of my own. No safety net. No off switch."

The recovery — 105 kg to 82 kg in three months through OMAD, 30K steps daily, and spiritual discipline — became the startup lesson: "You cannot be a 1% operator at work if you're careless with life." See [[personal-stories]] for the full account.

### Angel Investing

In his 2023 recap, Shravan noted: "Invested in 4+ startups of which 1 is at Series A." This is a quiet but growing part of the portfolio. The investing thesis aligns with the teaching thesis — back founders who show relentless initiative and genuine problem obsession. See [[angel-investing]] for the full portfolio.

### On AI and the Future of Startups

"AI PMs at these companies are pulling $500K+ packages." But Shravan's view is not that AI kills startups — it democratizes building. "Building in 2026 is not about who knows prompting. It's about who can think clearly with AI as a co-builder." This is why MPM Batch 7 was rebuilt as "Product x AI edition" — not adding AI as a topic, but weaving it into every PM decision.

### Great Hires as Startup Strategy

"A great hire enables other great hires which makes the company successful and that's what great org culture does, that's what great product culture promotes." Shravan's hiring philosophy prizes attitude over pedigree. The nervous candidate who went blank on a call but solved the problem with encouragement is now a GPM running a team of "super passionate folks not because they are from IIT, IIMs but because they have the ability to build something remarkable."

### Systems Thinking Over Hero Worship

"Most good product people are systems thinkers where they believe — it's not the person who is wrong and it's the system that is and they make efforts to change that." Applied to startups, the lesson is clear: do not build around heroes, build systems that make average people excellent. "You change the system, you change the node's behavior."

## Impact

Rethink Systems is the proof of concept. Built without funding, grown through content and community, scaled to India's premier PM program with a 100 Instructor NPS. The entrepreneurship philosophy is simple: build something you would want to exist, do the boring work daily, let the results compound. "It wasn't motivation that saved me. It was humility. Faith. And the refusal to quit."
