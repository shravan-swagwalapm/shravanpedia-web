> Srila Prabhupada's teachings and ISKCON — the spiritual foundation beneath everything Shravan builds.

## Context

This is not a hobby or side interest. **Prabhupada's teachings** are a foundational lens for Shravan. He built an entire RAG application ([[prabhupada-ai]]) to make these teachings accessible through AI. He has created ISKCON leadership presentations. The spiritual practice informs his work ethic, his teaching philosophy, and how he thinks about service. Across 71 LinkedIn posts that reference spirituality and the Gita, a clear pattern emerges: Shravan does not compartmentalize the spiritual and the professional. They are one system.

## Details

### PrabhupadaAI

The most direct expression of this devotion is a full **RAG web app** grounded in Prabhupada's teachings. Built on FastAPI and Next.js, deployed on Railway with SQLite and FAISS for vector search, and featuring ElevenLabs voice synthesis that lets users hear answers in Prabhupada's own voice, the app turns scripture search into a living, accessible experience. Pricing has been finalized and an admin console is planned. This is not a side project — it is the project with the deepest personal meaning in Shravan's entire portfolio.

### ISKCON Leadership

Shravan has created presentations for **ISKCON leadership** contexts, exploring the intersection of spiritual service and technology. The work reflects a recurring theme: that devotion and modern tools are not in tension but can amplify each other.

### The Gita as a PM Book

In every curated book list Shravan has ever posted — and he has posted the same list eight or more times from 2022 to 2026 — the **Bhagavad Gita As It Is** sits alongside *Inspired* by Marty Cagan, *High Output Management* by Andy Grove, and *Radical Candor* by Kim Scott. Never in a separate "spiritual" section. Always in "Miscellaneous" or the main list. The positioning is deliberate: this is not religion separate from work but a professional resource on par with the best PM literature.

When he built the "Ask SwagwalaPM" custom GPT, he described it as a bot that would "drop wisdom (maybe even a Bhagavad Gita quote) when you need it most." The Gita is woven into his teaching voice, not presented as a separate domain.

### Bhagavad Gita Principles in His PM Teaching

His Batch 6 graduation — 60+ students, a 7.5-hour final lecture — produced the statement that clearly moved him most: "This is not a product management program but a program deeply rooted in human values: resilience, persistence, and the principles of the Bhagavad Gita." He embraced this fully in his reflection: "After 6 amazing batches, I've realized that this community was never only about learning PM — it was always about something much bigger. It was about unlearning mistakes from the past, becoming a better human every day, forgiving yourself, and building a brighter future."

The Gita principles that surface most clearly in his teaching are **nishkama karma** (action without attachment to results) — expressed as "Be passionate about the problem space, not the compensation"; **resilience through dharma** — captured in "How you do anything is how you do everything"; **compassion as the foundation of action** — which he formalized into his "Compassion Funnel" framework for product thinking; and **humility before knowledge** — reflected in "Every conversation reminded me how much I still have to learn — and unlearn."

### Devotion in Public Posts

Shravan publicly attributes his success to God in his LinkedIn posts — not as a throwaway line but as the emotional anchor of his major reflections. His 2024 year-end post concluded: "My deepest gratitude to Lord Shiva, whose blessings have made everything possible. I remain, and will always be, his humble servant." During his 2025 health transformation, he wrote: "My mind slowed down. I started waking up at 5:30 AM. Chanting. Sitting quietly. Becoming more God-conscious." At the Rethink Systems Batch 3 graduation: "All my love and gratitude go to my wonderful students — and to my 'Mahadev,' who has made this program and community truly special."

The devotion is not performative. It intensified through his 2025 health crisis, when daily chanting and God-consciousness became central to his recovery. "It wasn't motivation that saved me. It was humility. Faith. And the refusal to quit."

### Compassion Funnel — Spirituality Applied to Product

His stray dog story from 2021 is the clearest bridge between spiritual values and product practice. Seeing an injured animal triggered compassion, which led to curiosity about the problem space, which led to passion for scaling a solution, which led to building a curated pet adoption marketplace. He named this the **Compassion Funnel** and framed it as the real source of product sense: "You don't need a lot of frameworks to become a better PM, you just have to be a better human to become a better PM." This is Gita philosophy in PM clothing.

### Service Orientation

The "Shravan Bhaiya" persona is **seva** (service) channeled through content creation and teaching. He started TheSwagWalaPM to make PM education free and accessible. More than 200 PMs transitioned at zero cost. The framing is always "yours" — every post ends with "Yours, Shravan Bhaiya." The relationship is not teacher-student but elder-brother-family. "Whatever I am today, it's because of you" appears repeatedly, echoing the Vaishnava principle of seeing oneself as servant, not master.

### Influence on Building

The spiritual foundation shapes the building in concrete ways: a service orientation that prioritizes tools serving others over pure monetization; patience and persistence that echo spiritual discipline; teaching as seva through @TheSwagWalaPM's knowledge sharing; and [[prabhupada-ai]] as the most direct expression of making Prabhupada's wisdom accessible via AI.

## Impact

Prabhupada's teachings are the "why" behind much of Shravan's work. PrabhupadaAI is the most direct expression, but the influence runs through everything — the Gita sitting alongside Marty Cagan in every book list, the Compassion Funnel framework, the devotional sign-offs, the students who said "you duped us into learning human values." The spiritual and the professional are not two tracks for Shravan. They are one practice, expressed through building products, teaching PMs, and showing up with humility every day.
