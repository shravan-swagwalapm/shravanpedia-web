> 20+ early-stage bets across 7 countries — healthtech, edtech, SaaS, consumer tech.

## Context

Shravan has been **angel investing** since December 2020, while still a Principal PM at BYJU'S. This is not a vanity portfolio assembled for LinkedIn bragging rights. It reflects the same thesis as [[rethink-systems]]: find scalable solutions for massive impact, back founders early, and stay long-term. The geographic spread across India, the United States, Canada, Pakistan, Bangladesh, and beyond is unusual for an Indian angel and reflects a genuine belief that great founders emerge from every ecosystem, not just the ones with the most developed venture infrastructure.

## Details

### Portfolio Overview

The portfolio includes **20+ startups** as of April 2026, spread across seven or more countries and focused on healthtech, edtech, SaaS, and consumer tech. Every investment is early stage, chosen not for quick returns but for the combination of a compelling problem, a founder who refuses to quit, and a solution that could scale to reach the people who need it most.

The growth from 4+ investments in 2023 to 20+ by 2026 tells a story of compounding conviction. The acceleration was not because Shravan suddenly had more capital to deploy. It was because each investment sharpened his pattern recognition, and the network effects of being India's top-ranked PM creator opened deal flow that a purely financial angel would never see. Founders in edtech and SaaS spaces seek him out because his platform — 194K+ LinkedIn followers, 45K+ YouTube subscribers — provides distribution value beyond the check. An investment from Shravan comes with a potential audience of hundreds of thousands of PMs who are exactly the users or customers many of these startups need.

### Named Investments

The known portfolio paints a picture of an investor who follows problems he understands deeply. **Foreign Admits** (study-abroad edtech) sits in the education space where Shravan has spent his career. **Airtribe** (cohort-based learning) mirrors the exact model he runs at Rethink Systems, giving him first-hand comparison data. **Growrk** (remote work equipment SaaS) addresses the infrastructure challenges of the distributed work era. **Maqsad** (leading edtech in Pakistan) represents his most geographically bold bet — backing education in a market where the need is immense and the venture infrastructure is nascent. **Go Paani** (water access and impact) is the furthest from a traditional angel thesis — a bet on a problem that matters, period. **MDplus** (healthcare platform) connects to the health reckoning he experienced in 2025, giving him a visceral understanding of why healthcare access matters. **Thekabook** (consumer) adds breadth. And **Cur8** (edtech, where Shravan serves as both Educator and Investor from 2023 to 2026) is the most distinctive investment in the portfolio.

### Investment Philosophy: Founder-First, Impact-Weighted

Shravan's investing philosophy mirrors his teaching philosophy at [[rethink-systems]] with striking precision. Just as he tells MPM students that "a PM should be founder material — not the founder, but definitely founder material who takes initiative as if it's his/her product," his investment thesis centers on the founder rather than the market.

The question he asks is not "is this a big market?" but "does this founder behave like someone who will figure it out regardless?" The 100% hike story from his own career — spending 15 hours writing a comprehensive PRD after a difficult interview, earning a callback and a 50% salary bump on top of what he expected — is the behavioral pattern he looks for in founders. Initiative that borders on unreasonable. Research depth that nobody asked for. The refusal to accept a "no" as final. He has written about the GPM running a team of "super passionate folks not because they are from IIT, IIMs but because they have the ability to build something remarkable." The same philosophy applies to his investing: he is looking for builders who would keep building even if nobody invested, because that persistence is the only reliable predictor of startup survival.

The impact weighting is genuine, not performative. The Maqsad investment in Pakistan's edtech space reflects a conviction that education is a universal problem worth solving regardless of where the returns are most predictable. Go Paani's focus on water access is about as far from a traditional angel thesis as possible — it is a bet on a problem that matters, backed by a founder Shravan believes in. The geographic spread is deliberately non-parochial. He backs founders wherever the problem is real, not just where the exit math is cleanest.

### The Cur8 Model: Investor as Operator

The Cur8 investment deserves special attention because it represents a model that is rare in angel investing and that maps directly to Shravan's broader philosophy. He is both investor and educator at Cur8, serving in both capacities since 2023. This is not passive capital with quarterly check-ins. He is in the product, in the curriculum, in the daily decisions. The dual role gives him pattern recognition that passive investors never develop — he does not just see the metrics, he sees the conversations that produced the metrics, the decisions that shaped the strategy, the moments where the founder's character determined the outcome.

This dual role feeds directly back into his cohort teaching at Rethink Systems. When he teaches growth loops and defensible growth (see [[growth-loops-and-defensible-growth]]), the examples include companies he has watched grow from pre-seed to Series A. When he teaches about founder mentality, he draws on conversations he had that week with founders in his portfolio — not articles he read three years ago, not Harvard Business School case studies, but live data from companies he is personally involved with.

### What He Looks For: Signals Over Credentials

Consistent with his [[career-and-hiring-views]], Shravan prizes attitude over pedigree in founders. The nervous candidate who went blank on a call but solved the problem with encouragement is the archetype he backs — not the polished MBA with a perfect deck. The distinction is critical: a polished pitch often masks shallow thinking, while genuine problem obsession often comes wrapped in nervous energy and imperfect communication. Shravan has learned to see through the presentation to the builder underneath.

The sectors he invests in — healthtech, edtech, SaaS, consumer tech — are not random. They are the sectors where his own experience provides the deepest judgment. His years at Edureka and BYJU'S gave him intimate knowledge of edtech economics: customer acquisition costs, lifetime value, retention curves, and the specific ways that edtech companies grow and the specific ways they die. His product roles at Flipkart and BlackBuck gave him consumer tech and logistics expertise. The healthtech investments connect to the health journey he lived through in 2025, which gave him a visceral understanding of why healthcare access matters — not as a market thesis but as a human reality. Every investment in the portfolio maps to a domain where Shravan has first-hand operational knowledge.

### Angel Investing as a Teaching Tool

The portfolio is not separate from the teaching — it is an extension of it. In the MPM cohort, Shravan can reference startup decisions from companies he is actively involved with, providing a level of specificity and recency that no external case study can match. When he teaches about pivots, he draws on pivot decisions he witnessed in real time. When he teaches about fundraising, he draws on term sheets he reviewed. When he teaches about the emotional reality of building — the loneliness, the ambiguity, the decisions that have no good option — he draws on conversations with founders who trusted him enough to be honest.

The investing also reinforces the [[content-flywheel]]: each investment adds a node to his professional network, and each node is a potential guest speaker for the cohort, a potential co-creator for LinkedIn content, a potential case study for the Builder's Bible, and a potential hiring partner for Rethink Systems graduates. The 20+ portfolio companies across seven countries represent not just financial bets but twenty-plus active relationships that compound the value of everything else Shravan builds and teaches.

## Impact

Angel investing gives Shravan pattern recognition across industries and geographies that feeds back into his own building and teaching. The diversity of the portfolio — from Pakistan's education system to remote work infrastructure to healthcare to water access — provides the kind of cross-domain perspective that enriches everything from his cohort curriculum to his product instincts. It is investing as learning, with financial returns as a secondary benefit. The growth from 4+ investments in 2023 to 20+ by 2026 reflects not a change in strategy but a compounding of the same thesis: back founders who show relentless initiative and genuine problem obsession, in sectors where your own operational experience gives you judgment that market analysis alone cannot provide. And the Cur8 model — investor as operator — proves that the deepest returns come not from writing a check and waiting but from getting close enough to the work that the patterns become visible.
