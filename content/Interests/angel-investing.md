> 15+ early-stage bets across 5 countries — healthtech, edtech, SaaS, consumer tech.

## Context

Shravan has been **angel investing** since December 2020, while still a Principal PM at BYJU'S. This is not a vanity portfolio — it reflects the same thesis as [[rethink-systems]]: find scalable solutions for massive impact, back founders early, and stay long-term. The geographic spread across India, the United States, Canada, Pakistan, and Bangladesh is unusual for an Indian angel and reflects a genuine belief in cross-border startup ecosystems.

## Details

### Portfolio Overview

The portfolio includes **15+ startups** since December 2020, spread across five countries and focused on healthtech, edtech, SaaS, and consumer tech. Every investment is early stage, chosen for long-term potential rather than quick returns.

### Named Investments

The known portfolio includes **Foreign Admits** (study-abroad edtech), **Airtribe** (cohort-based learning), **Growrk** (remote work equipment SaaS), **Maqsad** (leading edtech in Pakistan), **Go Paani** (water access and impact), **MDplus** (healthcare platform), **Thekabook** (consumer), and **Cur8** (edtech, where Shravan serves as both Educator and Investor from 2023 to 2026).

### Philosophy

The investing philosophy is early stage, long-term, and conviction-driven — not spray-and-pray. Each investment reflects a personal belief about the problem space. The cross-border approach is by design: Shravan backs founders wherever the problem is real, not just where the returns are most predictable. The **Maqsad** investment in Pakistan's edtech space and **Go Paani**'s focus on water access reveal a bias toward impact-first startups, not purely financial plays. **Cur8** is a hybrid — both investor and educator — which mirrors the Rethink model of skin-in-the-game teaching.

## Impact

Angel investing gives Shravan pattern recognition across industries and geographies that feeds back into his own building and teaching. The diversity of the portfolio — from Pakistan's education system to remote work infrastructure to healthcare — provides the kind of cross-domain perspective that enriches everything from his cohort curriculum to his product instincts. It is investing as learning, with financial returns as a secondary benefit.
