> 15+ early-stage bets across 5 countries — healthtech, edtech, SaaS, consumer tech.

## Context

Shravan has been **angel investing** since December 2020, while still a Principal PM at BYJU'S. This is not a vanity portfolio — it reflects the same thesis as [[rethink-systems]]: find scalable solutions for massive impact, back founders early, and stay long-term. The geographic spread across India, the United States, Canada, Pakistan, and Bangladesh is unusual for an Indian angel and reflects a genuine belief in cross-border startup ecosystems.

## Details

### Portfolio Overview

The portfolio includes **15+ startups** since December 2020, spread across five countries and focused on healthtech, edtech, SaaS, and consumer tech. Every investment is early stage, chosen for long-term potential rather than quick returns.

### Named Investments

The known portfolio includes **Foreign Admits** (study-abroad edtech), **Airtribe** (cohort-based learning), **Growrk** (remote work equipment SaaS), **Maqsad** (leading edtech in Pakistan), **Go Paani** (water access and impact), **MDplus** (healthcare platform), **Thekabook** (consumer), and **Cur8** (edtech, where Shravan serves as both Educator and Investor from 2023 to 2026).

### Philosophy

The investing philosophy is early stage, long-term, and conviction-driven — not spray-and-pray. Each investment reflects a personal belief about the problem space. The cross-border approach is by design: Shravan backs founders wherever the problem is real, not just where the returns are most predictable. The **Maqsad** investment in Pakistan's edtech space and **Go Paani**'s focus on water access reveal a bias toward impact-first startups, not purely financial plays. **Cur8** is a hybrid — both investor and educator — which mirrors the Rethink model of skin-in-the-game teaching.

### Investment Philosophy: Founder-First, Impact-Weighted

Shravan's investing philosophy mirrors his teaching philosophy at [[rethink-systems]] with striking precision. Just as he tells MPM students that "a PM should be founder material -- not the founder, but definitely founder material who takes initiative as if it's his/her product," his investment thesis centers on the founder rather than the market. The question he asks is not "is this a big market?" but "does this founder behave like someone who will figure it out regardless?" The 100% hike story from his own career -- spending 15 hours writing a comprehensive PRD after bombing an interview, earning a callback and a 50% salary bump on top of what he expected -- is the behavioral pattern he looks for in founders. Initiative that borders on unreasonable. Research depth that nobody asked for. The refusal to accept a "no" as final.

The impact weighting is genuine, not performative. The Maqsad investment in Pakistan's edtech space reflects a conviction that education is a universal problem worth solving regardless of where the returns are most predictable. Go Paani's focus on water access is about as far from a traditional angel thesis as possible -- it is a bet on a problem that matters, backed by a founder Shravan believes in. The geographic spread across India, the United States, Canada, Pakistan, and Bangladesh is unusual for an Indian angel and reflects a deliberately non-parochial worldview. He backs founders wherever the problem is real, not just where the exit math is cleanest.

The Cur8 investment is the most distinctive in the portfolio because it is a hybrid: Shravan is both investor and educator, serving as both since 2023. This mirrors the [[rethink-systems]] model of skin-in-the-game teaching -- he does not invest from a distance and wait for quarterly updates. He is in the product, in the curriculum, in the daily decisions. This dual role gives him pattern recognition that passive investors never develop, and it feeds directly back into his cohort teaching, where he can reference startup decisions he was personally involved in rather than case studies he read about.

### The Evolution from 4+ to 20+ Startups

The trajectory tells a story of compounding conviction. In his 2023 year recap, Shravan noted: "Invested in 4+ startups of which 1 is at Series A." By April 2026, per the rethinksystems.in website and LinkedIn data, the portfolio had grown to 20+ startups across seven or more countries. The acceleration is not because he suddenly had more capital. It is because the pattern recognition sharpened with each investment, and the network effects of being a recognized PM educator opened deal flow that a purely financial angel would not see. Founders in edtech and SaaS spaces seek him out because his platform (194K+ LinkedIn followers, 45K+ YouTube subscribers, India's top PM creator) provides distribution value beyond the check. An investment from Shravan comes with a potential audience of hundreds of thousands of PMs who are exactly the users or customers many of these startups need.

### What He Looks For: Signals Over Credentials

Consistent with his [[career-and-hiring-views]], Shravan prizes attitude over pedigree in founders. The nervous candidate who went blank on a call but solved the problem with encouragement is the archetype he backs -- not the polished MBA with a perfect deck. He has written about the GPM running a team of "super passionate folks not because they are from IIT, IIMs but because they have the ability to build something remarkable." The same philosophy applies to his investing: he is looking for builders who would keep building even if nobody invested, because that persistence is the only reliable predictor of startup survival.

The sectors he invests in -- healthtech, edtech, SaaS, consumer tech -- are not random. They are the sectors where his own experience provides the deepest judgment. His years at Edureka and BYJU'S gave him intimate knowledge of edtech economics. His product roles at Flipkart and BlackBuck gave him consumer tech and logistics expertise. The healthtech investments (MDplus) connect to the health crisis he lived through in 2025, which gave him a visceral understanding of why healthcare access matters. Every investment in the portfolio maps to a domain where Shravan has first-hand operational knowledge, not just market analysis.

### Angel Investing as a Teaching Tool

The portfolio is not separate from the teaching -- it feeds it. In the MPM cohort, Shravan can reference startup decisions from companies he is actively involved with, providing a level of specificity and recency that case studies from Harvard Business School cannot match. When he teaches growth loops and defensible growth (see [[growth-loops-and-defensible-growth]]), the examples include companies he has watched grow from pre-seed to Series A. When he teaches about founder mentality, he draws on conversations he has had that week with founders in his portfolio, not on articles he read three years ago.

The investing also reinforces the [[content-flywheel]]: each investment adds a node to his professional network, and each node is a potential guest speaker for the cohort, a potential co-creator for LinkedIn content, a potential case study for the Builder's Bible, and a potential hiring partner for Rethink Systems graduates. The 20+ portfolio companies across seven countries represent not just financial bets but twenty-plus active relationships that compound the value of everything else Shravan builds and teaches.

## Impact

Angel investing gives Shravan pattern recognition across industries and geographies that feeds back into his own building and teaching. The diversity of the portfolio -- from Pakistan's education system to remote work infrastructure to healthcare -- provides the kind of cross-domain perspective that enriches everything from his cohort curriculum to his product instincts. It is investing as learning, with financial returns as a secondary benefit. The growth from 4+ investments in 2023 to 20+ by 2026 reflects not a change in strategy but a compounding of the same thesis: back founders who show relentless initiative and genuine problem obsession, in sectors where your own operational experience gives you judgment that market analysis alone cannot provide.
