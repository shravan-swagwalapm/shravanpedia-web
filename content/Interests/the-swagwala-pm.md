> Shravan's YouTube brand — teaching PMs and non-devs to build with AI tools. Active since July 2021, 45K+ subscribers.

## Context

**@TheSwagWalaPM** is more than a channel — it is the distribution layer for everything Shravan builds and teaches. The name itself is a statement: a PM who actually ships. "Swag" here means the confidence that comes from building, not just managing — the quiet assurance of someone who has put their hands on the product, not just their opinions in the meeting room. The channel launched in **July 2021** during Shravan's time as Principal PM at BYJU'S, and it has since evolved through three distinct phases that mirror his own professional transformation from PM educator to builder-teacher to AI-native creator.

## Details

### The Origin Story

The channel began with a simple conviction: PM education in India was broken. The English-language PM creator ecosystem — Lenny Rachitsky, Shreyas Doshi, the Silicon Valley voices — produced brilliant content, but it was inaccessible to the vast majority of aspiring product managers in India. Expensive certifications guarded the gates. English fluency was an unspoken prerequisite. And the cultural context of building products in India — the Jugaad mentality, the multi-language user bases, the infrastructure constraints — was entirely absent from the Western curriculum.

Shravan decided to teach PM in **Hinglish** — the natural Hindi-English blend that millions of Indian professionals actually speak at work. The decision was both philosophical and strategic. Philosophically, it reflected his belief that knowledge should not be gatekept by language. Strategically, it carved a niche that no major PM creator occupied. The result was content that felt like a conversation with an older brother who happened to be a Principal PM at one of India's largest edtech companies — accessible, warm, grounded in real experience, and delivered in the language people actually think in.

The first videos were rough. There was no studio, no editor, no thumbnail designer. Just Shravan, a camera, and the accumulated knowledge of years spent building products at Flipkart, Edureka, BlackBuck, and BYJU'S. But the consistency was inhuman. Daily LinkedIn posts since April 2020. Weekly YouTube uploads from July 2021 onward. No gaps. No excuses. No viral hacks. Just the compound interest of showing up when nobody was watching, because the work itself was the reward.

### Content Pillars

The channel's content spans several interconnected pillars that together form a complete PM education. **PM fundamentals** — introductions to product management, how to get a first PM job, product thinking for decision-making — form the core curriculum that built the subscriber base. These videos are the entry point for thousands of aspirants, and they are designed to be rewatched. Shravan structures them as building blocks: each video stands alone, but together they form a progression from "what is PM?" to "how do I think about product strategy?"

**Product analytics and strategy** covers advanced material for working PMs: analytics frameworks, portfolio building, product strategy and vision. This is the content that keeps senior PMs coming back — not career-switch material, but the deep work of becoming a better operator.

**Live case solving** offers real-time product case walkthroughs in a "show your work" format that differentiates from textbook PM channels. Shravan does not present polished case study solutions. He thinks through problems live, makes mistakes, backtracks, and shows the messy reality of how product decisions actually get made. The format is deliberately vulnerable — it teaches the process, not just the answer.

**Claude Code workshops**, including the "Learn 90% of Claude Code in 90 minutes" multi-session series, represent the channel's boldest evolution: from PM education to AI-builder education. Session 2 covers git worktrees, team collaboration, CLI-to-app connections, and PR review workflows. These workshops draw a different audience — not just PMs but designers, marketers, and operators who want to build production software without waiting for an engineering team. The [[builders-bible]] serves as the 700+ page long-form companion, and [[shortstack]] is the planned AI-automated YouTube Shorts pipeline — seven videos per week using ElevenLabs voice clone, HeyGen, and Remotion.

### Content Series and Formats

The channel features a **15+ live session series** that serves as a comprehensive product management curriculum from introductory to advanced fundamentals — the backbone of the channel. Each session builds on the previous one, but the sessions are designed to be discoverable individually, so a new viewer can enter at any point and find immediate value.

Recurring content on **PM certifications and degrees** helps aspirants navigate the certification landscape — Shravan is characteristically blunt about which certifications are worth the money and which are credential-farming.

Practical **PM job search advice** offers the tactical content that originally built the audience — resume reviews, interview preparation, salary negotiation, and the unwritten rules of getting hired as a PM in India.

A deliberate content strategy of **meme-integrated teaching** uses humor to make PM concepts stick — this is how the SwagWalaPM brand differentiates from serious or corporate PM educators. The memes are not decoration. They are pedagogical tools: a well-placed Bollywood reference or a perfectly timed screenshot joke anchors an abstract concept in memory more effectively than any framework diagram.

### Audience

The channel serves four distinct but overlapping audiences. PMs who want to code — the growing cohort of product managers who understand that the line between PM and builder is dissolving. Non-devs learning to build real products — designers, marketers, and operators who see what Claude Code makes possible and want in. The Indian tech community, reached through [[hinglish-teaching]] explanations that make complex topics accessible in the language people actually think in. And PM job aspirants — the largest segment, more than 300 of whom have been helped into PM roles, with over 10,000 mentored overall.

### Channel Evolution

The channel has moved through three distinct phases that mirror Shravan's own professional transformation. **Phase 1 (2021-2023)** was pure PM education — breaking into PM, fundamentals, case studies. This built the subscriber base during Shravan's BYJU'S and Bhanzu years. The content was grounded in his daily experience as a working PM: the problems he solved on Monday became the teaching material on Tuesday. There was an authenticity to this phase that resonated deeply — viewers could tell this was not a consultant theorizing about PM but a practitioner sharing hard-won lessons in real time.

**Phase 2 (2024)** added the [[rethink-systems]] cohort funnel, turning the channel into the top-of-funnel for the $1,000/seat "Mastering PM 2.0" course. This phase was strategically important but did not change the channel's character. The free content remained free. The cohort content was additional, not replacement. Viewers who never paid a rupee continued to receive the same quality of education. The cohort was for those who wanted immersion, community, and direct access — not for those who needed the basics.

**Phase 3 (2025-present)** is the AI-builder pivot — Claude Code workshops, the Builder's Bible, and teaching non-devs to ship production software. This is the phase where the channel's identity crystallized into something entirely unique in the PM creator ecosystem. Other creators teach PM. Others teach coding. Others teach AI. Nobody else teaches a non-developer how to ship a production RAG application while explaining the product thinking behind every architectural decision. The "PM who builds" identity is fully realized, and it resonates because it reflects a real transformation — Shravan genuinely became a builder, and the channel documents that journey in real time.

### Tech Stack for Content

The content creation infrastructure reflects the same builder mentality that defines the channel's content. **ElevenLabs** provides Shravan's voice clone, powering the planned [[shortstack]] automated Shorts pipeline that will produce seven videos per week. **Excalidraw MCP** generates the system diagrams used in teaching multi-agent architectures — the same tool Shravan uses in his own building sessions, making the teaching an authentic window into his actual workflow. And the **Obsidian vault** powers the persistent context behind every session, ensuring that knowledge captured in one teaching moment is available in every subsequent one.

### The Name and the Brand

The name "SwagWalaPM" deserves its own explanation because it encodes the brand's entire philosophy. In Indian slang, "swag" is not vanity — it is earned confidence. "Wala" means "the one who has." A SwagWalaPM is not a PM who talks a good game. It is a PM who has the quiet confidence that comes from having shipped real products, solved real problems, and earned real scars. The name positions the brand against two common archetypes in PM education: the theoretical PM who has never shipped anything, and the celebrity PM who teaches from LinkedIn clout rather than building experience. The SwagWalaPM builds. That is the swag.
