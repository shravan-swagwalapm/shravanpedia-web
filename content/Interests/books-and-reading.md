> "I have an unreasonable number of books. Happy to trade recommendations." -- LinkedIn bio

## Context

Books are not a hobby for Shravan — they are infrastructure. He grew up as a voracious, undirected reader (Egyptian mythology, Hilbert's problems, Urdu mushairas) long before he found product management. His LinkedIn bio leads with books, not credentials. He curates and re-shares his reading lists obsessively, sometimes reposting the same book list across years with small updates, because he genuinely believes the right book at the right time changes careers.

His relationship with reading is deeply personal. As a kid in Jammu, while everyone expected him to study for boards, he was reading about the design of mathematics. In college at IIT Roorkee, he went to Urdu mushairas in Delhi instead of focusing on metallurgy. He calls himself a "generalist who loved doing whatever interested me" — and reading was the engine of that generalism.

## Details

### The Sacred Text

**The Bhagavad Gita As It Is** by A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada sits at the top of every list, always. Not in a "spirituality" section — right alongside product management books. He credits it with transforming his life: "In 2020, I read the BG, in which Lord Krsna spoke that compassion was the key to happiness, when you help others without expectation — bliss manifests." This was the book that broke his cycle of depression and imposter syndrome. It is not just a recommendation; it is the foundation of his entire life philosophy. See [[prabhupada-and-iskcon]] for how this text shapes his work.

### The PM Canon

Shravan has published detailed PM book lists repeatedly since 2022. The titles that appear most consistently form a clear canon. In **product thinking**, he returns again and again to *Creativity, Inc* by Ed Catmull ("putting 10x into practice"), *Made to Stick* by Chip and Dan Heath, *The Lean Product Playbook* by Dan Olsen ("one of the best books for learning product management for aspirants"), *In The Plex* by Steven Levy, and *Inspired* by Marty Cagan. For **product practice**, the essentials are *Continuous Discovery Habits* by Teresa Torres, *Getting To Yes* ("the best book on negotiation I have seen"), *Sprint* by Jake Knapp, and *Getting Things Done* by David Allen. In **product leadership**, *High Output Management* by Andy Grove ("still a classic"), *Empowered* by Marty Cagan, and *Product Leadership* by Banfield and Eriksson anchor the shelf.

His **design thinking** recommendations center on *Don't Make Me Think* by Steve Krug, *The Design of Everyday Things* by Don Norman ("still a classic, will always be a classic"), and *The Design Thinking Playbook* by Michael Lewrick. For **culture and people**, he recommends *Radical Candor* by Kim Scott, *No Rules Rules* by Reed Hastings, and *The Captain Class* by Sam Walker. And then there are the wildcards — non-PM books he insists PMs must read: *On Writing* by Stephen King ("PMs need to be great writers"), *The Hard Thing About Hard Things* by Ben Horowitz, *Team of Rivals* ("one of the best books written on how to influence without authority"), *Start With Why* by Simon Sinek, and *Chatter* by Ethan Kross.

### The Extended Library

In a May 2022 post, he published a much deeper list organized by discipline — Analytics, Business, Design, Engineering, Product Management — recommending 30+ books including *Blue Ocean Strategy*, *Algorithms to Live By*, *100 Things Every Designer Needs to Know About People*, *Behave* by Robert Sapolsky, *Chaos: Making a New Science* by James Gleick, *The Mythical Man-Month* ("old yet relevant"), *Escaping the Build Trap* by Melissa Perri, and *Business Model Generation* by Alexander Osterwalder. Honorable mentions that he references but does not always include in the essential lists are *Hooked* by Nir Eyal, *Crossing the Chasm*, and *Built to Last*.

### Reading as Identity

His childhood reading pattern reveals something important: he was never a disciplined reader. He read what fascinated him — mythology, mathematics, music, religion — and the breadth became his superpower. "I was always a generalist who loved doing whatever interested me." Reading across domains is what eventually made him a PM who can teach with examples from Age of Empires, the Bhagavad Gita, Korean kindness, and startup salary negotiations in the same breath.

### Articles He Recommends

Beyond books, Shravan curates essential article lists that he reshares regularly: "In Defense of Eye Candy" (design), "TikTok and the Sorting Hat" (product), "The Psychology of Design" from A List Apart, "How Superhuman Built an Engine to Find Product/Market Fit", and "What Buddhism Taught Me About Product Management" by Lenny Rachitsky.

## The Library (435 Books — Apple Books)

In April 2026, a full inventory of Shravan's Apple Books library confirmed the "unreasonable number" claim: **435 books**. The complete categorized collection reveals far more about who he is than any LinkedIn bio could.

### Spiritual / Philosophy / Eastern Thought (35+ books)

The largest category by emotional weight, spanning Hindu, Buddhist, Sufi, Stoic, and mystical traditions — not as academic study but as lived practice. The foundational text is *Bhagavad-gita As It Is* by Prabhupada (see [[prabhupada-and-iskcon]]). The collection extends through *Bhagavad Gita In the Light of Kashmir Shaivism*, *The Journey Home* by Radhanath Swami, *Autobiography of a Yogi* by Paramahansa Yogananda, multiple Buddhist texts (*In the Buddha's Words*, *What the Buddha Taught*, *Old Path White Clouds*), Kashmir Shaivism (*The Secret Supreme*), Sadhguru (*Inner Engineering*, *A Taste of Well-Being*), contemplative works (*The Power of Now*, *The Untethered Soul*, *The Surrender Experiment*), Sufi poetry (*The Poetry of Kabir*, *Mirabai: Ecstatic Poems*), and Japanese warrior philosophy (*Hagakure*, *The Book of Five Rings*).

### Stoicism / Western Philosophy (20 books)

A full Stoic library — not just the popular titles but primary sources. Two copies of *Meditations* by Marcus Aurelius sit alongside *Letters From a Stoic*, *Enchiridion*, and Ryan Holiday's complete shelf (*The Daily Stoic*, *Ego Is the Enemy*, *The Obstacle Is the Way*, *Stillness Is the Key*, *Discipline Is Destiny*, *Lives of the Stoics*). The collection also includes *The Consolation of Philosophy*, *Thus Spoke Zarathustra*, *The Courage to Be Disliked*, and *Xenophon's Cyrus the Great*.

### Product Management / Business (35+ books)

The professional toolkit, with every major PM framework represented: *High Output Management*, *Radical Candor*, *Inspired*, *Zero to One*, *The Lean Startup*, *Working Backwards*, *The Hard Thing About Hard Things*, *Creativity Inc*, *No Rules Rules*, *Blitzscaling*, and *Good to Great*. The AI-specific shelf includes *The AI Product Manager's Handbook* and *The AI Product Playbook*. Interview prep is covered by *Cracking the PM Interview* and *Decode and Conquer*. Growth and metrics are represented by *Radical Focus*, *Hacking Growth*, and *Traction*.

### Science / Math / Physics (30+ books)

This is where it gets unusual for a PM. Fifteen books on prime numbers, infinity, chaos theory, and mathematical biography. *Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman* appears twice — a hero. The shelf includes *The Man Who Knew Infinity* (Ramanujan), *Fermat's Last Theorem*, *Perfect Rigor* (two copies), *Prime Obsession*, *The Music of the Primes*, *God Created The Integers*, *A Mathematician's Apology*, *Is God a Mathematician?*, and *Chaos: Making a New Science*. For a PM who studied metallurgy, this collection is deeply unusual and points back to the kid in Jammu who was reading about Hilbert's problems instead of studying for boards.

### Sci-Fi / Fiction (17 books)

Curated, not sprawling. Mostly idea-driven fiction — the kind that changes how you think. *Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy*, *Slaughterhouse-Five*, *Brave New World*, *Animal Farm*, *Atlas Shrugged*, *Snow Crash*, *Rendezvous with Rama*, *Solaris*, *Brothers Karamazov*, *Stories of Your Life and Others* by Ted Chiang. No beach reads. Every novel on the list is the kind that reshapes how you model the world.

### Psychology / Behavioral Science (19 books)

### History / Geopolitics / Biography (30+ books)

Heavy on India, Israel, North Korea, and individual genius. The India shelf includes *The Last Mughal*, *City of Djinns*, *Our Moon has Blood Clots* (Kashmir — a personal connection; see [[personal-stories]]), *Bhagat Singh: Why I Am An Atheist*, and *Field Marshal Sam Manekshaw*. ### Self-Help / Productivity / Mindset (50+ books)

The largest category by count — the operating system. *Atomic Habits*, *The 4-Hour Workweek*, *Getting Things Done* (two copies), *Range*, *Principles* (two copies), *Extreme Ownership*, *Never Split the Difference*, *Clear Thinking*, *The Almanack of Naval Ravikant*, and *Ikigai* are among the highlights of a shelf that spans habits, focus, meaning, negotiation, and life design.

### Systems Thinking (13 books)

Not a subsection of something else — a distinct intellectual commitment. *Thinking in Systems* by Donella Meadows, *The Systems Bible* (two copies), *The Fifth Discipline*, *Out of Control* by Kevin Kelly, *Limits to Growth*, and *Consilience*. See [[systems-thinking]] for how this maps to his building and teaching philosophies.

### Additional Shelves

The collection also includes marketing and growth books (7, led by *Ogilvy on Advertising* and *Purple Cow*), writing and creativity (6, anchored by *On Writing* by Stephen King), and a surprisingly deep humor shelf (14 books, with Oscar Wilde appearing three times).

### What the Library Reveals

The PM books are tools; the rest is who he is. Product management represents maybe 35 of 435 books — under 10%. The spiritual collection (35+), mathematics shelf (30+), and systems thinking library (13) dwarf the professional section. This is a person who became a PM because of how he thinks, not a person who thinks the way he does because of PM.

Mathematics is an obsession, not a curiosity. Fifteen books on prime numbers alone. Two copies of *Perfect Rigor*. For a PM who studied metallurgy, this points directly back to the kid in Jammu reading about Hilbert's problems instead of studying for boards.

The heroes are visible through the duplicates: Feynman (2 copies), Keanu Reeves (2 books), Oscar Wilde (3 books), Marcus Aurelius (2 copies), Kevin Kelly (2 books across categories), Ryan Holiday (6 books — the complete shelf). These are not casual reads; these are people he returns to.

Systems thinking is a worldview, not a skill — thirteen dedicated books from Donella Meadows to Kevin Kelly to complexity theory. India runs through everything — Kashmir, Delhi, Chanakya, Bhagat Singh, Kabir, Mirabai. The spiritual shelf is multi-tradition but rooted in one: Hindu texts dominate, but Buddhist, Sufi, Celtic, Japanese, and Stoic traditions branch out from that center. And the fiction shelf is entirely idea-driven — Ted Chiang, Lem, Heinlein, Huxley, Dostoevsky, Clarke. Every novel changes how you model the world.

## Impact

Books are Shravan's curriculum. His entire teaching philosophy at Rethink Systems — first principles, empathy, systems thinking — traces back to specific books. The Bhagavad Gita reshaped his life. The PM canon shaped his career. The wildcards shaped his voice. His advice to students: "Spend 50 percent of your time reading PM books but spend 50 percent of your time doing what you truly enjoy."

Reading, for him, is not preparation for work. It is the work. The 435-book Apple Books inventory confirms what the LinkedIn posts hinted at: the professional shelf is a small fraction. The real library — spiritual texts, mathematics, systems theory, behavioral science, history — is the infrastructure of a generalist mind that happens to do product management.
