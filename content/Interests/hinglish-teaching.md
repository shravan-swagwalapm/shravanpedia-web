> Teaching tech in Hindi-English mix — the way Shravan Bhaiya explains it on a YouTube live.

## Context

**Hinglish** is not just a language choice — it is an accessibility decision. Shravan's audience is Indian PMs and non-devs. Technical concepts explained in pure English create distance. Hinglish creates familiarity. When "Shravan Bhaiya" explains Claude Code, it feels like a friend teaching you, not a professor lecturing at you.

## Details

### What Hinglish Teaching Looks Like

The style mixes Hindi sentence structure with English technical terms, grounded in relatable Indian cultural references and analogies. The energy mirrors a live YouTube session — conversational, not scripted. "Bhai, yeh dekho" always beats "Please observe the following." The technical vocabulary stays in English because that is how the terms are actually used in Indian workplaces, but the explanation wrapping around those terms is Hindi, which is the language the audience thinks in.

### Why It Works

The effectiveness comes down to cognitive load. When the framework of the explanation is familiar (Hindi), the listener's mental energy goes entirely toward understanding the new concept (the English tech term), rather than also processing an unfamiliar communication style. It builds trust — "this person speaks my language" translates directly to "this person understands my context." And it is viral in Indian tech circles precisely because Hinglish content feels authentic in a space dominated by English-only creators who often feel distant from the lived experience of most Indian tech professionals.

### Implementation

This approach is codified in a custom Claude Code skill (`/hinglish-explain`) that can explain any concept in Shravan's Hinglish style. Workshop scripts use Hinglish for explanation and English for code, creating a natural bilingual flow. The [[builders-bible]] is written entirely in English because written technical content benefits from the precision of a single language, but the accompanying video content on [[the-swagwala-pm]] is delivered in Hinglish — spoken and written media each get the language that serves them best.

## Impact

The language choice is itself a product decision. Hinglish is the distribution advantage that makes @TheSwagWalaPM reach people that English-only tech content simply cannot. It is accessibility through language — the same instinct that drives all of Shravan's teaching.
