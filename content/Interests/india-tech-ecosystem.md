## Context

With 831 posts tagged India — his largest thematic bucket alongside career advice — Shravan is deeply embedded in the **Indian tech ecosystem**. He is an IIT Roorkee graduate, held PM roles at Indian startups like Blackbuck, founded what many call "the best PM program in India," speaks at IITs across the country, and angel invests in Indian startups. India is not just where he works — it is the soil everything grows in.

## Details

### IIT and the Inferiority Complex

Shravan's most personal India story begins in 2011 when he joined **IIT Roorkee** for Metallurgy — not IIT Bombay, not IIT Delhi, not Computer Science. "I used to disgust with the fact that I was studying at IIT Roorkee instead of IIT B or D and had my discipline as 'Metallurgy' and not 'CS'." He lied about which IIT he attended. His rehearsed answers were always grander than the truth.

The thought of leveling up according to what others saw as success drove him, and the imposter syndrome grew subconsciously. After four job changes and a well-compensated PM role, he by all external measures should have been happy — but he was depressed and lonely. The breakthrough came from the **Bhagavad Gita** in 2020: "Lord Krsna spoke that compassion was the key to happiness, when you help others without expectation — bliss manifests." The IIT tag stopped mattering. "The only thing I cared about was how I can help the next person better." See [[personal-stories]] for the full account.

### The Indian PM Ecosystem

Shravan sees India's PM ecosystem as underdeveloped relative to the talent pool. That gap is exactly what [[rethink-systems]] fills. The Swag Wala PM channel became "the largest individual PM channel in India." He has lectured at IIT Madras, IIT Kharagpur, IIT Roorkee, and NTU Singapore. He was the first PM speaker at the PMI Pearl City Hyderabad chapter. Rethink alumni now work at Google, Microsoft, Swiggy, 1mg, Meesho, Tata 1mg, and startups across India. He has spoken to more than 3,000 product aspirants directly or indirectly — from the US, India, Canada, and Australia.

### Indian Fintech and Startups as Case Studies

Shravan's curated reading lists reveal what fascinates him about India's tech story. He repeatedly recommends "Indian Fintech: Still a Cub but Roaring Like a Tiger" as essential reading. He tracks how global giants approach the Indian market through pieces like "Decoding ByteDance's Strategy to Tap India's Music App Market." He studies network effects built on India's unique phone-number culture through "Truecaller's Growth Machine: Reaching 300M Users." And he follows the attention economy through "Zuckerberg Explains Why TikTok Has Become Bigger Than Instagram In India." These are not casual shares — they are the case studies he weaves into his teaching.

### Korea Story — The Indian Abroad

In 2014, as a research intern at **KAIST** in South Korea, Shravan was alone, sick, broke, with no working phone and no one who spoke English. "I sat in a park crying in pain and agony." A Korean woman — a complete stranger — bought him medicine, food, and stayed until he recovered. He still calls her every three to four months. This experience shaped his entire approach to teaching: "Teaching PM for me wasn't the point — it was teaching folks how to be better humans and learn the attitude of gratitude." See [[personal-stories]] for the full story.

### The Bangalore Hub

The [[rethink-systems]] retreats happen in **Bangalore**, the center of India's PM community. Cohort 3's two-day graduation retreat achieved an NPS of 80+. The ecosystem is centered there — Swiggy, CRED, Cashfree, and most of the Indian PM community Shravan interacts with daily call Bangalore home.

### The Indo-Pak Product Ecosystem

Shravan created a video on "How product management is driving innovation in the Indo-Pak startup ecosystem" — a notably rare take. He sees product thinking as a **South Asian opportunity**, not just an Indian one. This perspective is reflected in his [[angel-investing]] portfolio, which includes Maqsad, Pakistan's leading edtech platform.

### Education as India's Leverage

"Future of Edtech in India" is a recurring theme in his YouTube content. Rethink Systems itself is the thesis in action — that cohort-based, community-driven education can outperform both traditional institutions and scalable-but-shallow online courses. "Beyond products, building leaders" is the tagline, and it is specifically about India's next generation of product operators.

### Compassion Culture vs. Hustle Culture

The Indian tech ecosystem often glorifies hustle. Shravan pushes the opposite: "In the end, it's your attitude that will make you special, not some myriad of skills." His founding story — from imposter syndrome to health crisis to recovery through faith and discipline — is a counter-narrative to the "grind harder" culture that dominates Indian LinkedIn. "And trust me there is more good in this world than bad."

## Impact

India is not just the market — it is the mission. [[rethink-systems]] exists because the Indian PM ecosystem needed something that combined world-class thinking with genuine compassion. The numbers speak: 400+ operators, 150+ transitions, NPS scores that are "unprecedented in the product management ecosystem." The four-part journey Shravan describes — from mimicking the world to self-reflection to commitment to legacy — is both his personal arc and the arc he sees India's tech ecosystem going through. The talent is here. The ambition is here. What was missing was someone saying "I am there for you" — and meaning it.
